# :unlock: M0DUL0

Independent hardware hacking community based in Malaysia. 🇲🇾

> Context: M0DUL0 is a Malaysia-based independent hardware hacking community comprised of students and professionals alike. As a team, we conduct hardware security research, develop proof-of-concepts (PoCs), and perform hardware modifications across various platforms.

This repo covers how M0DUL0 runs: mission, conduct, ethics, and contributing. Read it before you publish under the M0DUL0 name.

## :dart: Mission

Hardware security is hard to get into. Most resources are scattered, assume prior knowledge, or are buried in conference talks and research papers with no clear starting point. M0DUL0 exists to fix that: a community where anyone, regardless of background, can find structured hardware hacking resources, learn from real research, and build practical skills alongside people who've already done it.

## 🛠️ How We Do It

We organise our resources, materials, research papers, and projects around traditional CTF categories. Not because hardware hacking requires playing CTFs, but because those categories give people a familiar structure to map unfamiliar concepts onto:

- **OSINT** :detective: datasheet hunting and teardown research.
- **Reversing** :gear: board-level reverse engineering and firmware disassembly.
- **Pwn** :boom: fault injection and glitching.
- **Crypto** :closed_lock_with_key: side-channel analysis and key extraction.
- **Forensics** :mag: firmware dumping and chip-off recovery.
- **Hardware** :wrench: UART, JTAG/SWD, RF/SDR, BLE, CAN bus, ICS/SCADA.

The categories are a lens, not a prerequisite. You don't need a CTF background to use them.

## What M0DUL0 Is

- A hands-on community: research, CTFs, and hardware PoCs.
- Open to anyone serious about learning, beginner or expert.
- Low-ego and credit-where-it's-due. If you wrote it, your name is on it.

## What M0DUL0 Is Not

- Not a platform for testing or modifying devices you don't own or have permission to touch.
- Not an authority that authorises research against third-party hardware, networks, or production systems.
- Not a source of legal advice. Research laws vary by jurisdiction.

## Joining M0DUL0

New members join through Discord via our [Linktree](https://linktr.ee/modulo). Once you're in, Discord's onboarding flow walks you through self-assigning your experience level and field of interest: CTFs, Researcher, Modifier, or any combination. Access to the community opens automatically once roles are set.

## Community Practice Priorities

- Legality and consent first.
- Ethics by default.
- Responsible disclosure over public dumps.
- Reproducible writeups and source integrity.
- Professional conduct and community safety.

## :handshake: How to Contribute

1. :globe_with_meridians: Stay active across our platforms: LinkedIn, Discord, and more.
2. :checkered_flag: Compete in CTFs regularly. It builds M0DUL0's visibility and credibility.
3. :microscope: Join research sessions. We're big on hardware PoCs, mods, and hands-on work.
4. :pencil: Contribute writeups and white papers to our GitHub repos, with full credit attached.
